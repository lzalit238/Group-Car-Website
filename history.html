1965 Shelby Mustang GT350 & 1966 Shelby GT350

These cars were the smallest and lightest cars of the GT350 models. The "R" varients weighing 75 pounds less than the normal GT350. They were built specifically for racing and this model was a champion for three years straight.

1967 Shelby GT350/ GT500

There were plans to introduce a convertible during production but they fell through due to financial and supply problems. One 1967 Fastback was updated to a GT500 with a racing engine producing 650 hp. It was known as the "Super Snake".

1967-1968 Shelby Cobra GT350

The 1967 model was heavier than its predecessor because of added features, including a longer hood. It also had new taillights and ten-spoke, fifteen-inch, cast-aluminum rims. It started out as a stock Mustang with a 4-speed manual transmission.

2006; 2007 Ford Shelby GT-H

This Ford version of the Shelby GT-H Mustang had a paint job that mimicked that of the original GT350H from 1966. 500 of these cars were made to celebrate the 40th anniversary of the original Shelby GT350H and they were only avalible at Hertz car rental agencies.

2013-2014 Ford Shelby GT350 & GT500

This was presented as an upgrade kit for the Mustang GT and was built in both coupe and convertible body models. It was built 6-speed automatic. The engine could with a manual gearbox and turned into a standard.

2016 Ford Shelby GT350 & GT350R

This car was more track-focused and it was the first mass-produced car to ever be fitted with carbon fiber wheels. Extra pieces including the backup camera and stereo could be removed to make the car lighter.
